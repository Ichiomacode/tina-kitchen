Varieties of Local Dishes

Amala and Ewedu

A traditional Yoruba dish consisting of Amala (yam flour) served with Ewedu soup.

Pounded Yam

A delicious dish made by boiling yams and then pounding until smooth, usually served with a variety of soups.

Catfish Peppersoup

A spicy symphony of flavors with catfish, aromatic broth, and Nigerian culinary artistry.

Jollof Rice

A popular West African dish made with rice, tomatoes, and a blend of flavorful spices.

Eforiro

A delicious Nigerian spinach stew made with a variety of vegetables and often served with rice or fufu.

Moi Moi

A steamed bean pudding made from grounded peeled beans, onions, and other spices.

Suya

A popular Nigerian street food made with thinly sliced spicy grilled meat.

Akara

Deep-fried bean cakes made from black-eyed peas, onions, and spices.

Ofada Rice

A variety of rice grown in Nigeria, often served with a special pepper sauce and assorted meats.
